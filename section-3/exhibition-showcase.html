What's Happening

On "Gimmickiness"

Viewed in isolation, some elements‚Äîthe dramatic countdown, the alarming colour shift‚Äîmay feel theatrical. This is intentional. The installation operates through emotional demonstration, not intellectual explanation. The "gimmick" is the pedagogy.

  • Theatre serves critical function: making invisible harms visible
  • The countdown creates "consent retrospection"‚Äîrecognising what you agreed to
  • Dramatic elements work within the full experiential context
  • Real-world testing will refine emotional calibration

Ethical Framework

The installation creates the experience of violation without causing actual harm. All data processing occurs locally in the browser‚Äînothing leaves the user's device during the demonstration. The simulated threat of public exhibition teaches through safe exposure.

  • No data is collected, stored, or transmitted
  • The "upload" is theatrical, not functional
  • Only genuinely consented, anonymised excerpts reach the gallery
  • University ethics approval guides all development
üéØ

Critical Intention

You Agreed. isn't anti-technology‚Äîit's pro-comprehension. The critique targets consent mechanisms designed for reversible data (cookies) being applied to irreversible extraction (AI training). The goal is to make visitors feel what they already agreed to elsewhere.

  • Demonstrates the gap between legal and genuine consent
  • Makes cognitive extraction tangible and visible
  • Positions visitors as complicit, then aware
  • Advocates for consent frameworks that match extraction stakes
