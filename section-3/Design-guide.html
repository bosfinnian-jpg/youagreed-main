Conceptual Framework

Design Philosophy

The visual identity of You Agreed deliberately employs the aesthetic vocabulary of contemporary tech interfaces — clean, vibrant, inviting. This isn't accidental. The colour palette and typography mirror the designed environments we encounter daily in digital spaces: the friendly blues of social platforms, the soft pastels of consumer apps, the approachable sans-serifs of modern web design.

This creates a critical tension: the work uses the visual language of the systems it critiques. The aesthetic that typically signals trust, accessibility, and user-friendliness here becomes a tool for revealing how those same design choices facilitate surveillance and extraction.

The pastels aren't hostile or dystopian — they're the same colours that surround our daily digital engagement. The typography isn't ominous — it's the readable, accessible typeface of legitimate platforms. This is intentional: surveillance capitalism doesn't look threatening. It looks polished, professional, even beautiful.

By adopting this aesthetic, the installation doesn't ask viewers to imagine a different world — it asks them to see the one they already inhabit with fresh recognition.
