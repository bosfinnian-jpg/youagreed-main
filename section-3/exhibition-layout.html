Spatial Configuration & Atmospheric Design

The installation occupies the Leeds television studio, a controlled environment that enables precise manipulation of sensory elements. Upon entry, visitors encounter a deliberately constructed atmosphere: a low-frequency technical hum establishes an ambient soundscape reminiscent of data processing infrastructure, whilst programmable LED strips generate slow-transitioning gradients of cool blue and white light.

This creates a clinical yet ethereal environment - sterile enough to evoke server farms and computational spaces, atmospheric enough to feel immersive rather than hostile.

The aesthetic vocabulary references the designed interfaces of surveillance capitalism: clean, sophisticated, seductive. This is not the dystopian darkness of fictional surveillance states, but rather the polished veneer of contemporary digital architecture.

Interface Architecture: Dual Entry Points

Visitors immediately confront two monitors positioned to create a deliberate choice architecture:

Station One: Terms and Conditions Display
A vertical monitor presents extensive legal documentation - dense clauses rendered in the standardised language of digital consent frameworks. The text scrolls through hundreds of provisions, each legally precise and practically unreadable. Header text instructs users to "PLEASE READ CAREFULLY BEFORE PROCEEDING." The volume and density are calibrated to induce the exact cognitive response these documents produce in daily digital life: the immediate impulse to scroll to acceptance without genuine comprehension.
Station Two: Conversational Data Analyser
An interactive interface invites visitors to input mock AI conversation data for immediate analysis. The system visualises extraction patterns: communication styles, emotional indicators, cognitive vulnerabilities. The interface is deliberately engaging, offering the gratification of insight and the illusion of transparency. Users receive immediate feedback, creating a stark contrast with the adjacent legal display.

The Critical Integration: Mandatory Consent

Here's the essential intervention: To access the Analyser, users must first interact with the Terms and Conditions display. They must physically scroll to the document's conclusion and click "I AGREE" - replicating the exact muscle memory of digital consent we've all developed. This isn't optional. The analyser remains locked until consent is given.

This creates the exhibition's central mechanism: visitors perform the very act the work critiques. They experience the friction of lengthy terms, the desire to skip to functionality, and ultimately the click-through consent that characterises contemporary digital engagement.

Primary Display: Navigable Surveillance Topology

The 150-inch screen functions as the exhibition's architectural centrepiece. At this scale, the display transitions from viewed object to environmental space. The rendered world employs Three.js and WebGL to create an explorable 3D landscape populated with translucent "brain" structures - particle-based installations representing cognitive data collection points.

These structures aren't merely decorative; they form a navigable topology of extraction. Each installation corresponds to specific data categories: communication patterns, emotional analysis, behavioural prediction models. They pulse with subtle luminosity, creating what appears as a constellation of neural architecture suspended in digital space.

A drivable car provides navigation through this landscape, controlled via Xbox Adaptive Controller. The physics implementation is deliberately weighted - movement feels grounded rather than frictionless, creating a sense of embodied navigation through abstract data space.

Guided Experience Protocol

The operator serves as both guide and interpreter, positioned at the controller station. The initial demonstration follows a structured path:

1
Introduction
Welcome visitors and introduce analyser functionality with visualisation of extraction mechanisms
2
Navigation
Guided tour through 3D environment, contextualising each data structure and collection point
3
Connection
Explanation of relationship between navigable world and analysed conversation data
4
Exploration
Transfer control to visitors for self-directed exploration of the data landscape

Following visitor exploration, the critical reveal occurs: The operator directs them back to the Terms and Conditions display and navigates to Section 11, which stipulates that analysed data may be incorporated into public exhibition. This staged moment creates visceral recognition - the realisation that they consented to precisely what they're now witnessing, without comprehension or genuine choice.

Enhanced Sensory Design

Soundscape Layering
Beyond the foundational technical hum, the audio environment incorporates barely perceptible whispered fragments: excerpts from AI training prompts, cookie policy language, terms of service clauses. These elements sit just below conscious recognition, creating subliminal density - the sense of being surrounded by linguistic systems that operate whether we attend to them or not.
Responsive Lighting Architecture
The LED system integrates reactive behaviours tied to system states:

During data analysis: Subtle pulsing, slight colour temperature shifts towards cooler tones
During navigation: Gentle gradients that track movement through the 3D space
During the reveal: Lighting stabilises, becomes more clinical

This responsive design makes the physical space itself feel observant, reactive - surveillance rendered as environmental quality rather than discrete watching.

Theoretical Implementation

The exhibition operates as a functioning demonstration rather than merely a representation. Visitors don't just learn about consent mechanisms - they enact them. They don't observe surveillance aesthetics - they navigate through them. The installation creates complicity before comprehension, mirroring the exact sequence of contemporary digital engagement.

This approach advances Zuboff's surveillance capitalism framework from theoretical critique to embodied experience. The gap between legal consent and informed understanding isn't explained but felt - in the impulse to skip the terms, in the gratification of the analyser, in the beauty of the explorable world, and finally in the uncomfortable recognition of what was agreed to without awareness.

The work doesn't rely on shocking revelations or heavy-handed messaging. Instead, it creates conditions for self-recognition: the moment when visitors understand they've just performed, in miniature, the exact pattern of behaviour that enables large-scale cognitive extraction.
