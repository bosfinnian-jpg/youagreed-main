Spatial Purpose

The Leeds television studio provides a controlled environment where visitors physically enact the consent mechanisms they critique. The space uses ambient sound, programmable lighting, and clean visual design to reference surveillance capitalism's aesthetic: not dystopian darkness, but the polished, seductive interfaces of contemporary digital platforms.

Two-Station Consent Architecture

Station One: Terms and Conditions
A vertical monitor presents extensive legal documentation in standardised consent language. Hundreds of provisions scroll past, each legally precise and practically unreadable. Header instructs: "PLEASE READ CAREFULLY BEFORE PROCEEDING." Volume and density calibrated to induce the exact response these documents produce daily: immediate impulse to skip to acceptance.
Station Two: Data Analyser
An interactive interface analyses mock AI conversation data, visualising extraction patterns: communication styles, emotional indicators, cognitive vulnerabilities. Deliberately engaging, offering immediate feedback and the illusion of transparency.

Mandatory Consent Mechanism

To access the Analyser, users must first scroll through the Terms and click "I AGREE". This isn't optional. The analyser remains locked until consent is given, replicating the exact muscle memory of digital consent we've all developed.

Visitors perform the very act the work critiques, experiencing the friction of lengthy terms, the desire to skip to functionality, and the click-through consent that characterises contemporary digital engagement.

150" Display: Navigable Data Landscape

The primary screen displays an explorable 3D environment representing cognitive data extraction. Structures correspond to data categories (communication patterns, emotional analysis, prediction models). A drivable car, controlled via Xbox Adaptive Controller, allows visitors to navigate this abstract representation of their data being processed and categorised.

The Reveal Moment

After guided navigation through the 3D environment and visitor exploration, the operator directs participants back to the Terms display. Section 11 stipulates that analysed data may be incorporated into public exhibition.

This staged moment creates visceral recognition: visitors realise they consented to precisely what they're witnessing, without comprehension or genuine choice. The reveal doesn't shock through fictional scenarios but through self-recognition of actual behaviour.

Embodying Surveillance Capitalism Theory

The installation operates as functioning demonstration rather than representation. Visitors enact consent mechanisms rather than observing them, creating complicity before comprehension. This mirrors contemporary digital engagement where agreements precede understanding.

The work advances Zuboff's surveillance capitalism framework from theoretical critique to embodied experience. The gap between legal consent and informed understanding isn't explained but felt, in the impulse to skip terms, in the analyser's gratification, and finally in the uncomfortable recognition of unaware agreement.
