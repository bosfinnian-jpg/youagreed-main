AI-Assisted Development: The Reflexive Element

This project operates within the University of Leeds' "AI Green" assessment category, which explicitly encourages generative AI use as integral to the work. The methodological approach creates deliberate tension: I am building a critique of AI data extraction using AI assistance to overcome technical limitations.

Without AI assistance, this project simply wouldn't exist. The technical implementation: interactive data parsing, countdown mechanics, sophisticated animations, responsive design, unfortunately, exceeds my current coding capabilities. AI tools translate conceptual vision into functional code, allowing me to focus on what matters: critical framework, user experience design, and educational impact.

What AI handles: technical implementation of defined requirements, code debugging and optimisation, animation generation based on my specifications, and architectural recommendations for features I've designed.

What remains mine: conceptual design and critical framework development, theoretical grounding in surveillance capitalism literature, user experience architecture and interaction design, visual direction and atmospheric decisions, written analysis, physical installation design and all of the execution.

The reflexive layer: Using AI to critique AI surveillance creates productive meta-commentary. I benefit from this technology's democratising potential while recognising that these same systems operate through the consent theatre mechanisms I'm exposing. This tension demonstrates that the question isn't "is AI beneficial or harmful?" but rather: "What are the terms of exchange, do users understand those terms, and are current privacy frameworks adequate when extraction becomes permanent and irreversible?"

My position: AI represents genuinely transformative capability and should be used. But it cannot be allowed to replicate the surveillance capitalism model where extraction happens invisibly. Privacy regulations designed for reversible cookie tracking are simply inadequate for irreversible AI model training involving permanent cognitive pattern extraction.
